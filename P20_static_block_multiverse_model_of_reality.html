The following statements summarize my current worldview:

The most macroscopic (i.e. “zoomed out”) level of existence is a static (i.e. unchanging) and timelessly eternal (i.e. with neither a temporal beginning nor a temporal end) “block multiverse” which contains every superposition of every phenomenon.

Every phenomenon has a noumenal counterpart but not every noumenon necessarily has a phenomenal counterpart.

A phenomenon is a finite allocation of space, time, energy, and sometimes matter which exactly one frame of reference renders such that the phenomenon is distinguishable from other phenomena and from the “invisible field” of all noumena.

A noumenon is basically a superposition of a particular phenomenon (and a superposition is an abstract “object” representing multiple distinguishable configurations of a particular phenomenon (and such configurations are physically impossible to render within a single frame of reference simultaneously)).

An example of a superposition is a variable, X, which represents both the binary digit zero (i.e. 0) and the binary digit one (i.e. 1) simultaneously.

Within the all-encompassing “block multiverse” referred to as nature exist “growing block universes” (and a “growing block universe” is a space-time-energy continuum which appears “from inside of” that continuum (from the perspective of a frame of reference which renders phenoma) to be amassing more information, temporal length, and sometimes also spatial volume and total energy as time elapses within that continuum.
